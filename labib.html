The Greenhouse Effect

An overview of how the Earth's atmosphere traps heat and its impact on climate change.

What is the Greenhouse Effect?

The greenhouse effect is the process by which certain gases in the Earth's atmosphere trap heat, keeping the planet warm enough to support life.

Causes of the Greenhouse Effect

The main gases that contribute to the greenhouse effect are carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O). Human activities such as burning fossil fuels and deforestation have significantly increased the concentrations of these gases.

Impacts of the Greenhouse Effect

Increasing greenhouse gases are contributing to global warming, melting ice caps, rising sea levels, extreme weather events, and disruption of ecosystems.

How to Reduce the Greenhouse Effect?

Solutions include transitioning to renewable energy sources (solar, wind), reducing emissions, protecting forests, and promoting sustainable farming practices.
