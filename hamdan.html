Introduction to Global Warming

Understanding the Impact and What You Can Do


What is the Global warming?

Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities, primarily the release of greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), and nitrous oxide (N₂O) into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat from the Sun, creating a "greenhouse effect" that warms the planet.

Impact of global Warming

As global temperatures increase, polar ice caps and glaciers are melting at an accelerated rate, contributing to higher sea levels. Additionally, the warming of ocean water causes it to expand, further raising sea levels. This rise in sea levels can lead to several adverse effects.

Impacts of the Globalwarming

  • Rising sea levels due to melting ice caps.
  • More extreme weather events, such as hurricanes, heatwaves, and floods.
  • Loss of biodiversity as species struggle to adapt to changing conditions.
  • Disruption of ecosystems and agricultural systems.
